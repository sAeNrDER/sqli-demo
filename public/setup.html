STEP 1

Create a PortSwigger Account

PortSwigger Academy hosts the official SQL injection labs used in this unit. You need a free account to access them. The whole process takes under 2 minutes.

1

Go to the registration page

Open your browser and navigate to:

https://portswigger.net/users/register
2

Fill in your details

Enter your email address and choose a password. Use your university email if possible — it keeps your account identifiable for future labs.

Email yourname@student.monash.edu Password something you'll remember ↓ see warning below Username anything you like
3

Verify your email

PortSwigger will send a verification email to the address you used. Open your inbox and click the confirmation link. Check your spam folder if it doesn't arrive within 1–2 minutes.

4

Log in and go to the labs

Once verified, log in and navigate to Web Security Academy:

https://portswigger.net/web-security/sql-injection
⚠️ Important — Save Your Password Right Now

PortSwigger's login system uses a one-time email link (magic link) after you register. Every time you log in from a new device or session, PortSwigger emails you a login link instead of asking for a password.

This means:

  • Each login link expires after one use — you can't click it twice
  • If you close the tab before the lab loads, you'll need a new link
  • In the lab room, open the email link immediately — don't leave it sitting in your inbox
  • Keep your inbox open in another tab during the lab session
Tip: Once you're logged in and a lab is open, don't close that tab. PortSwigger keeps your session active as long as the lab window stays open.
🔑 Lab Credentials — Write Them Down Immediately

When you launch a PortSwigger lab, the lab page shows you a set of generated credentials for that specific lab session — for example:

Username: wiener Password: pq7hz3m2kf9t Note: These are randomly generated for your session — yours will be different
  • These credentials are generated once per lab session — they're not shown again
  • If you navigate away and come back, the credentials panel may be gone
  • Write them in your notes immediately when the lab opens
  • They expire when the lab session ends — a new session gives new credentials
Where to find them: The credentials box is shown in the lab description panel at the top of the lab page, usually labelled "Your credentials" or "Log in with". Look for it before you start clicking anything.

STEP 2

Configure Burp Suite Proxy

You have two options for routing traffic through Burp. Option A is the easiest — use Burp's built-in browser which requires zero configuration. Option B is for your regular browser.

RECOMMENDED Option A — Use Burp's Built-in Browser

Burp Suite comes with its own Chromium-based browser that is pre-configured to route all traffic through Burp's proxy. No manual proxy settings, no certificate issues.

1

Open Burp Suite

Launch Burp Suite Community Edition. On the startup screen, click "Temporary project" → "Use Burp defaults" → "Start Burp".

2

Open the built-in browser

In Burp Suite, go to the Proxy tab → click "Open browser". A Chromium window opens — this is your attack browser.

💡 The built-in browser's traffic automatically appears in Proxy → HTTP history — no setup needed.
3

Navigate to the lab in the built-in browser

In the Burp browser, go to your PortSwigger lab URL. All requests will be logged in Burp's HTTP history automatically.

4

Turn on Intercept when you're ready to modify a request

Go to Proxy → Intercept. Click "Intercept is off" to toggle it on (button turns orange). Now any request made in the browser will pause here for you to modify before forwarding.

Intercepting: Intercept is on
Browsing normally: Intercept is off
ALTERNATIVE Option B — Configure Your Browser Manually (Firefox)

If you prefer to use your regular browser, you need to point it at Burp's proxy and install Burp's CA certificate. Firefox is the most reliable choice for this.

Part 1 — Point Firefox at Burp

1

Verify Burp's proxy listener

In Burp Suite: Proxy → Proxy settings → Proxy listeners. Confirm there's a listener on 127.0.0.1:8080 with Status = Running.

2

Open Firefox proxy settings

In Firefox: ☰ Menu → Settings → search "proxy" → Settings… (under Network Settings at the bottom of the General page)

3

Set Manual proxy configuration

Select "Manual proxy configuration" and fill in:

HTTP Proxy: 127.0.0.1 Port: 8080 Also use for HTTPS: ✓ checked

Click OK to save.

Part 2 — Install Burp's CA Certificate (for HTTPS)

Without the certificate, Firefox will show a security error for every HTTPS site. This installs Burp's certificate so it can decrypt and re-encrypt HTTPS traffic transparently.

4

Download the certificate

With the proxy configured and Burp running, navigate to this address in Firefox:

http://burpsuite

Click "CA Certificate" to download cacert.der.

5

Import the certificate into Firefox

In Firefox: ☰ Menu → Settings → search "certificates" → View Certificates → Authorities tab → Import…

Select the cacert.der file you just downloaded. Tick "Trust this CA to identify websites" → OK.

6

Verify it works

Navigate to https://portswigger.net in Firefox. If the page loads without a security warning and you see the request appear in Burp's HTTP history — you're all set.

Remember to reset the proxy when you're done. While the proxy is active, Firefox routes ALL traffic through Burp — including your normal browsing. When you finish the lab, go back to Firefox proxy settings and select "No proxy" or "Use system proxy settings".
